Social Media Optimization Research

Social media optimization (SMO) refers to the optimization of a website or elements of a website and its content for mass social consumption (Kenton). SMO could be used as a digital strategy in order to increase awareness of new products and services on a website in the purposes of connecting more customers and reform potential damaging news (Kento). So the way SMO works is that it leads the pubic from social websites to company website where people can find more information regarding to the company (Kento). According to Rudder, social media can be optimized follow nine steps:

  • 1. Generating a Social Media Style Guide by reviewing or writing the mission statement that defines how a business aims to meet customers’ needs. Building brand personal that should be conveyed in how business engage followers and customers. Making sure key components such as voice, tone, color palette, design components and logo usage policies are aligned with the mission statement.
  • 2. Analyzing competitors helps business to understand what customers want and how to provide it to them.
  • 3. Creating goals based on competitor analysis.
  • 4. Researching for keywords and hashtags by determining which words and phrases are most searched for content in the industry.
  • 5. Optimizing social media profiles by taking advantage of researched data to reconfigure the business name, images, bio information and business information to target popular search terms.
  • 6. Improving website social media sharing by including the right social media button.
  • 7. Focusing on creating quality and relevant content on the website - a business’s goal should be to produce content unique to the brand that meets the goals established by research and analysis.
  • 8. Targeting new audiences by taking advantage of social media platforms that allow businesses to promote posts beyond just immediate followers.
  • 9. Always update SMO strategy - A SMO strategy worked couple months ago may not be effective today as customer expectations and needs always change
